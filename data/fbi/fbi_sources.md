## What FBI Data Measures
The FBI Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR) / National Incident-Based Reporting System (NIBRS) program collects hate crime data from law enforcement agencies across the U.S. An incident is counted only when:
1. A law enforcement agency receives the report
2. The investigating officer determines there is sufficient evidence of bias motivation
3. The agency participates in and submits to the federal program

**Bias category used:** Anti-Asian (a subcategory of Anti-Race/Ethnicity/Ancestry)

## Key Methodology Notes
- **UCR → NIBRS transition (2021):** The FBI retired the Summary Reporting System (SRS) in favor of NIBRS in January 2021. This reduced the number of participating agencies from ~15,138 (2020) to ~11,834 (2021), causing a data discontinuity. The 2021 spike in anti-Asian incidents is real but may be partially affected by agency composition changes.
- **Voluntary participation:** Not all U.S. law enforcement agencies participate. In 2023, 16,009 agencies participated (covering ~95.2% of population).
- **Massive undercount:** The Bureau of Justice Statistics' National Crime Victimization Survey (NCVS) estimates that only ~54% of violent hate crimes are reported to police, and of those, only a fraction are classified and submitted as hate crimes. Actual incidence is far higher than FBI figures.
- **Definition:** FBI counts *criminal* offenses only. Non-criminal harassment, discrimination, and avoidance are outside FBI scope.
