## What STOP AAPI Hate Measures
STOP AAPI Hate is a coalition launched March 19, 2020 by Chinese for Affirmative Action, the Asian Pacific Policy and Planning Council (A3PCON), and San Francisco State University's College of Ethnic Studies.

The reporting center at **stopaapihate.org** allows community members to self-report hate incidents directed at Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders. Reports include:
- Criminal offenses (assault, vandalism, harassment)
- Non-criminal acts (verbal abuse, deliberate avoidance, shunning, discrimination)
- Online hate
- Civil rights violations (employment, housing, services)

**The platform captures what law enforcement does not:** the full spectrum of anti-Asian hostility experienced in daily life, most of which never enters any official record.

## The Taxonomy Revision (November 2023)
In November 2023, STOP AAPI Hate retroactively reclassified all incidents from 2020–2022 using a new hate acts taxonomy that distinguishes between explicit racial bias, coded bias, and perceived bias. This changed their own published numbers:

| Year | Original figure | Revised figure | Difference |
|------|----------------|----------------|------------|
| 2020 | 4,632 | 4,409 | −223 |
| 2021 | 6,273 | 5,679 | −594 |
| 2022 | not previously published annually | 1,321 | — |

Neither set of numbers is wrong. They reflect different frameworks for what "counts." The revision is its own data point: even the organization that collected the data changed how it categorized it, three years later.

## The 2022–2024 Reporting Shift
After 2021, STOP AAPI Hate moved away from publishing detailed annual incident counts and toward population-level surveys (conducted with NORC at the University of Chicago). The steep drop in reported incidents (5,679 → 1,321 → 735) is partly methodological, partly organizational, and partly unknown. The organization has not published a full explanation. The surveys show hate *experience* remaining high (49–53% of AAPI adults annually), even as the incident count in the reporting center declined.

## Key Methodology Notes
- **Self-reporting:** All data is community-submitted via an online form. Reports are not verified by law enforcement.
- **Voluntary:** Only individuals who are aware of the platform and choose to report are counted. Massive undercount of actual incidents.
- **Non-criminal acts included:** Unlike FBI, STOP AAPI Hate counts verbal harassment, social exclusion, and discrimination — which make up the majority (63%) of reported incidents.
- **Demographics:** Approximately 2/3 of reports are filed by women. Non-binary respondents report higher rates of online harassment.
- **Age:** Elderly victims (61+) are disproportionately subject to unprovoked physical assault. Youth (18–29) reported the highest overall rate of hate experience in 2024 (74%).
- **Geography:** Reports come from all 50 states + D.C., with concentration in states with large AAPI populations (California, New York, Washington, Texas, Illinois).
- **Language:** Reporting form available in multiple Asian languages to reduce language barriers.

## Population Survey Data (2024, NORC Partnership)
A separate methodology — population survey rather than incident reports — found:
- **53%** of all AAPI adults experienced some form of anti-AAPI hate in 2024
- **74%** of AAPI adults aged 18–29 reported anti-AAPI hate in 2024
- Survey conducted with NORC at the University of Chicago for statistical rigor
